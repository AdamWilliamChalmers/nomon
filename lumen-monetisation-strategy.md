# Lumen — Monetisation Strategy
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** June 2026  
**Status:** Active anchor — update as product evolves

---

## Core principle

Free is not a compromise. Free is the strategy.

A tool that sits quietly in your browser and nudges you toward better thinking cannot charge you a monthly fee to keep doing it. Subscription would make Lumen nag you about paying for the thing that's supposed to stop you being nagged. That's philosophically incoherent, and users would feel it even if they couldn't name it.

The model: **free core, one-time depth unlock, cohort licensing for institutions.**

No subscriptions. No data sales. No enterprise procurement cycles until the product earns them.

---

## The three layers

### Layer 1 — Free, forever
**When:** From day one  
**Revenue:** None  
**Goal:** 1,000 weekly active users

The entire core product is free with no artificial limits:

- All four signals (Loop, Drift, Mismatch, Depth)
- Ghost mode, always available and prominent
- Basic weekly digest
- Goal setting and tracking
- 30 days of session history

This is not a loss leader. This is the product. The free experience must be genuinely complete — not a crippled version designed to annoy people into upgrading. If the free tier feels like a demo, the philosophy is broken.

**The only job at this stage is engagement, not revenue.** Get the extension into the hands of people who use AI daily and care about their own thinking. Measure weekly retention, not downloads.

---

### Layer 2 — Lumen Deep (one-time purchase)
**When:** Once weekly retention is established across a few thousand users  
**Price:** £39 one-time (no recurring charge, ever)  
**Goal:** Validate willingness to pay before building anything complex

A single one-time unlock for users who want to go further. Not a subscription. Not a tier. You pay once, you own it.

**What Deep unlocks:**

| Feature | Free | Deep |
|---|---|---|
| Signal detection (all four) | Full | Full |
| Ghost mode | Always | Always |
| Session history | 30 days | 12 months |
| Weekly digest | Basic summary | Full longitudinal charts |
| Goal tracking | Up to 3 goals | Unlimited, with pattern analysis |
| Data export | — | Full personal data export (CSV/JSON) |
| Reflection journal | — | Linked to sessions, searchable |

**Why one-time, not subscription:**

- Matches the product philosophy — pay once for the mirror, it doesn't charge you to keep looking
- Removes the recurring guilt/friction that conflicts with "mirror not nanny"
- Simpler to build and operate at early stage
- Strong signal on genuine willingness to pay (one-time buyers are real buyers)

**Pricing logic:** £39 is below impulse-purchase threshold for a professional tool used daily. It is not trying to be cheap — it's trying to be fair. Revisit if conversion data suggests room to move.

---

### Layer 3 — Cohort licensing
**When:** Once the product has credibility and you're presenting/teaching with it  
**Price:** £500–2,000 per cohort depending on size and duration  
**Goal:** Meaningful unit economics without enterprise sales cycles

Institutional licensing for structured learning contexts — not enterprise SaaS, not "AI for your whole company." Specific cohorts with a defined purpose.

**Target contexts:**

- MBA / executive education courses on AI literacy
- Law firm or consulting firm responsible-AI training programmes
- University modules where AI use is being actively examined
- Professional development cohorts (accountants, doctors, policy analysts)

**What a cohort licence includes:**

- Lumen Deep for every participant for the duration of the cohort
- Cohort-level aggregate dashboard (opt-in only, privacy-first) showing group-level signal trends
- A facilitated reflection session template (can be run by the course leader, not Lumen)
- Named acknowledgment in any research outputs if cohort data is used

**Why this fits:**

- Sales cycle is weeks, not months — a professor or L&D lead can decide and purchase without procurement
- Natural fit with Edinburgh, Q-Step, and the existing academic network
- Every cohort is a case study, a testimonial, and a pipeline for Deep upgrades from participants
- Directly aligned with the product's intellectual positioning

**What a cohort licence does not include:**

- Individual-level data for the institution — participants' personal signal data remains private to them
- Ongoing account management or enterprise support (keep this lean)
- Custom feature development (this is a licence, not a consulting engagement)

## What this model is not

**Not a data business.** Aggregate insight sales are a trust landmine. The moment you're selling data about how people think to third parties, you are one journalist away from a crisis that destroys the only asset the model runs on — user trust. The longitudinal dataset is valuable as a product moat and research credibility signal. It is not a revenue line.

**Not freemium with artificial limits.** The free tier is not designed to frustrate. It is not missing features that a normal user would expect. The line between free and Deep is about depth of history and analysis, not about access to core protection.

**Not enterprise SaaS.** Team dashboards, SSO, compliance logs, and custom integrations are a different company with a different sales motion. Don't build toward that until the core product is proven and you have a specific enterprise customer pulling you there.

**Not subscription.** See above.

---

## The data flywheel

The longitudinal dataset builds itself as users engage. It does not need to be a revenue line to be valuable.

What it does:

- Makes signal detection more accurate over time
- Provides the empirical basis for research papers and academic credibility
- Enables the "State of Human-AI Thinking" report as a brand/PR asset (not a product)
- Creates calibration studies (post-session survey + anonymised counts) that strengthen the research moat

**Privacy architecture (non-negotiable):**

- Research contribution is **on by default** — users opt out any time in the extension (Privacy & data). Daily counts and optional feedback snippets only; never full chats
- Users can view exactly what is shared via a transparency dashboard
- Full deletion on request, within 30 days
- No individual-level data ever leaves the product — only aggregate, differentially private outputs
- Annual independent audit, published publicly

This is not a compliance checkbox. It is the foundation of the trust that makes everything else work.

## Sequencing

**Phase 1 — Build trust, not revenue**  
Ship the extension. Get to 1,000 weekly active users. Measure retention obsessively. Do not add monetisation. Do add Ghost mode, signal accuracy improvements, and a weekly digest people actually look forward to.

**Phase 2 — Test willingness to pay**  
Introduce Lumen Deep as a one-time purchase. Measure conversion rate among engaged users (weekly active, 4+ weeks). Target: 5–10% conversion among that cohort. If you hit it, the model works. If you don't, investigate why before scaling.

**Phase 3 — First cohort licence**  
Run one cohort — most likely Edinburgh-adjacent, where the network exists. Treat it as a learning exercise as much as a revenue exercise. Refine the facilitation template and the aggregate dashboard before selling a second one.

**Phase 4 — Scale what's working**  
By this point you have real data on which stream is growing. Double down on that. Do not try to run all three streams at equal intensity — pick the one with the best unit economics and strongest pull.

## Strategic risks

**Risk:** The user base doesn't reach the scale needed for the data flywheel to be meaningful.  
**Mitigation:** Deep and cohort licensing don't require scale to work. They require engagement. Focus on a smaller, genuinely active user base rather than growth-hacking downloads.

**Risk:** One-time pricing undervalues the product over time.  
**Mitigation:** Early adopters get the one-time price. Revisit pricing for new users as the product matures. One-time pricing is a launch mechanic, not a permanent ceiling.

**Risk:** Cohort licensing becomes a consulting trap — institutions want custom features.  
**Mitigation:** The licence is explicitly scoped. Document what's included and what isn't. Charge for custom work at a consulting day rate if it's valuable enough to do, but don't let it pull product development off course.

**Risk:** A well-funded competitor ships a similar product and goes free + VC-subsidised.  
**Mitigation:** The moat is the longitudinal data and the research credibility — not the feature set. A competitor can copy the signals; they cannot copy two years of calibration data and a body of peer-reviewed work.

## What success looks like at 12 months

- 5,000+ weekly active users
- 10%+ conversion to Lumen Deep among 4-week retained users
- 2–3 cohort licences completed, with a case study from each
- One piece of published research or credible public writing grounded in Lumen data
- Zero trust incidents
