UI Redesign · Mockups · v1

A quieter, more premium Nomon — one mark, three clear jobs.

Direction: Aurora Grayscale. Surfaces go near-black (or warm paper) with soft light gradients; the signature four-dot mark stays the only saturated colour in the product. Everything else earns attention through contrast, weight and space — not a rainbow of pills.

Toggle Ink / Paper top-right to preview both host themes (dark sites like Gemini vs. light sites like ChatGPT / Claude).

Colour is the mark, only

The four dots carry the brand — the sole saturated colour in the product. Signals, cards and panels stay grayscale and differentiate by label, weight and space.

Light on ink

Soft radial gradients and inner highlights give depth without chrome. Glass surfaces, hairline borders, generous radii.

Three jobs, always legible

Mirror · Badge · Cost. Each pillar owns one glyph, one purpose line, and one place in the FAB and panel — no colour needed.

01 — The mark & FAB

The four-dot mark stays. The FAB finally says what it does.

At rest it's a silent brand pill. On hover/tap it expands into a three-pillar rail — Mirror, Badge, Cost — each with a glyph, a one-line purpose, and a live status dot. No more guessing what the dots are for.

03 — Transparency badge

Surface the disclosure where the reply is — not buried in settings.

Below any substantial AI reply, a quiet "Disclose how you used AI" affordance. Tapping opens an inline card (not a modal) showing the inferred level with its evidence, three one-tap options, and a copy-ready badge.
